The Kepler Mission

The Kepler Mission, launched by NASA in 2009, was designed to discover Earth-like planets orbiting other stars. Using a space telescope, Kepler observed over 150,000 stars in a fixed field of view to detect the tiny dips in brightness caused by planets transiting their host stars.

Kepler's primary mission ran until 2013, followed by the K2 mission until 2018, resulting in the discovery of thousands of exoplanets. Its data has been instrumental in advancing our understanding of planetary systems and the potential for life beyond Earth.

This project leverages Kepler's open datasets to train AI/ML models for automated exoplanet detection, allowing users to explore and predict exoplanet candidates interactively.
